Undergrad Thesis

A Method for Eliminating Language Differences Between English and French Language | PDF | Github


Abstract

This dissertation investigates approaches to improve the quality of French-to-English machine translation through the use of linguistic features including lemma, part-of-speech (POS) identifiers, and named entity recognition (NER). In contrast to conventional evaluative methodologies, the objective of this study is to enhance the precision and consistency of translations by judiciously incorporating linguistic insights.

The effectiveness of linguistic feature integration in improving the performance of machine translation is examined, making use of the vast Common Corpus dataset. The study aims to investigate the distinct contributions of linguistic features in enhancing the quality of translations for the French and English languages, the importance of incorporating language characteristics into machine translation frameworks, methodological structures for integrating linguistic characteristics, and observable improvements in translation quality resulting from the integration of linguistic features.

This study makes a valuable contribution to the ongoing discussion on improving machine translation in multilingual environments by judiciously incorporating linguistic features. It provides significant insights into the ways in which linguistic enhancements can bolster the precision and consistency of translations, which have far-reaching implications for the fields of computational linguistics, natural language processing, and cross-cultural communication.

Keywords: Linguistic features; Machine translation; French; English; Common Corpus; Transformers.
